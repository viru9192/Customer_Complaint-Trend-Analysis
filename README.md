# 📊 Customer Complaint Trend Analysis

## Project Overview
Customer complaints provide valuable insight into how financial products and services perform in real-world scenarios. This project analyzes **62,000+ consumer complaints** to identify patterns in customer dissatisfaction, understand the most common issues faced by consumers, and evaluate how effectively companies respond to these complaints.

The objective of this analysis is to transform raw complaint data into **actionable insights that can help organizations improve operational processes and customer experience.**

---

## Business Problem
Financial institutions receive thousands of customer complaints every year. However, simply collecting complaint records does not automatically reveal the underlying problems customers are experiencing.

This project aims to answer key business questions such as:

- Which financial products generate the highest number of complaints?
- What issues are customers reporting most frequently?
- Are complaints concentrated in specific geographic regions?
- How do customers submit complaints?
- How effectively are companies responding to these complaints?

Understanding these patterns can help organizations identify operational weaknesses and prioritize improvements.

---

## Dataset
The dataset contains **62,516 consumer complaints related to financial products and services.**

Each complaint record includes information such as:

- Product category
- Issue reported by the customer
- Sub-issue
- Customer state
- Complaint submission channel
- Company public response
- Whether the complaint received a timely response
- Complaint submission date

These attributes allow analysis across **products, issues, regions, and time trends.**

## Data Preparation
Before performing the analysis, several preprocessing steps were carried out:

- Handling missing values in sub-product, sub-issue, and response fields
- Cleaning and standardizing categorical variables
- Converting complaint submission dates into structured time components
- Creating additional variables for time-based analysis

These steps ensured the dataset was clean and suitable for reliable analysis.

## Key Insights

### 1️⃣ Products Generating the Most Complaints
The majority of complaints are concentrated within a few financial products:

| Product | Complaints |
|------|------|
| Checking / Savings Accounts | 24,814 |
| Credit Card / Prepaid Card | 16,197 |
| Credit Reporting Services | 7,710 |
| Mortgage | 6,601 |

These products represent the largest sources of customer dissatisfaction.

---

### 2️⃣ Most Common Customer Issues
The analysis revealed that a small number of issues account for the majority of complaints:

- Managing an account – **15,000+ complaints**
- Incorrect information on credit report – **~4,900 complaints**
- Disputed transactions on statements – **~4,400 complaints**
- Account closure issues

This indicates that **account management and credit reporting systems require operational improvements.**

---

### 3️⃣ Geographic Distribution
Complaints are geographically concentrated in several large states:

- California
- Florida
- New York
- Texas

These states consistently reported the highest complaint volumes across multiple product categories.

---

### 4️⃣ Complaint Submission Channels
Most customers submitted complaints through **online channels**, indicating the growing importance of digital customer support systems.

---

### 5️⃣ Response Performance
While most complaints received timely responses, response delays varied depending on:

- Issue complexity
- Product type
- Regional service differences

This suggests opportunities to improve **complaint triaging and escalation processes.**

---

## Business Recommendations
Based on the analytical findings, the following improvements are recommended:

- Improve usability of **digital banking platforms**
- Strengthen **credit reporting verification systems**
- Simplify **account management and closure processes**
- Implement **automated complaint prioritization systems**
- Enhance **customer support capacity in high-complaint regions**

These actions could significantly reduce customer dissatisfaction and improve service quality.
